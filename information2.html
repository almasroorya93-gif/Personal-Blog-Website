    • Beaches, plains and mountains Oman is characterized by the diversity of its nature, from plains, mountains, and beaches that attract tourists from all over the world, and the most important of these areas that tourists visit: Green Mountain:
    The Green Mountain is located in the state of Nizwa, and it is characterized by its agricultural diversity, from flowers and trees, such as: almond trees, walnuts, pomegranates, as well as peaches, and apricots, in addition to roses that do not grow in any region in the Arabian Gulf except in the Green Mountain, and the height of this mountain reaches 3000 meters, and it has a mild climate in summer, and cold in winter.
    Mughsil Beach: Mughsail Beach, located in the Wilayat of Salalah, is characterized by its soft silver sand, which the visitor can see from the Marneef Cave, where water fountains rise from the bottom of the rocks, emitting a loud roar sound that entertains its visitors when visiting it.
    Atin Plain: The Atin Plain is located in the Dhofar Governorate, specifically in the city of Salalah, and it is one of the beautiful places frequented by visitors in the autumn season to relax and recuperate, due to its beautiful atmosphere, picturesque green meadows, and wonderful trees, and near the Atin Plain there is an important eye, which is Ain Jarziz, which is a source of water wealth in Dhofar.

    Markets When visiting the Sultanate of Oman, it is necessary to visit the markets in it, which are various markets, some of which are old, and some of which are modern, and the following is a mention of the most important markets in the Sultanate of Oman:
    Sohar Historical Market: The historical market (Sohar Market) located in the state of Sohar, in the Hujrah area, has been restored, with an area of 4575 m2The market has four gates:
    The first gate is located on the northern side, connecting the market to the old buildings, and its end reaches the creek.
    The second gate is located on the southern side of the market and extends to Oman International Bank, passing through the mosque and buildings in the area.
    The third gate is located on the eastern side, along the sea street.
    The fourth gate is located on the western side, and is called (the gate of the room).
    Nizwa Heritage Market: The Nizwa Heritage Market is one of the oldest and most important markets in the Sultanate of Oman, as it is a comprehensive and large market, and it is a market rich in its authentic plant and animal products, where residents go to Nizwa Market on Fridays significantly, because it is considered the most important day to visit, in addition to its importance in the period between Eid Al-Fitr and Al-Adha, due to the cheap prices of livestock and sheep.

    Castles and fortresses The Sultanate of Oman is characterized by its castles and majestic fortresses that indicate its historical depth, and these castles are a tourist destination that cannot be overlooked when visiting Oman, and the most important of these castles and fortresses are: Khasab Fort: Built by the Portuguese in the seventeenth century, Khasab Fort is a distinctive and wonderful fortress due to its charming location, overlooking the eastern part of the Wilayat of Khasab.
    Muttrah Fort: Muttrah Fort is located in the Governorate of Muscat, and dates back to the sixteenth century, and was built on a plateau top overlooking the beach, and has three large circular towers.

    Archaeological sites and museums Visitors to the Sultanate of Oman must enjoy visiting its museums and archaeological sites that are spread in various regions in it, and the most important of these sites and museums are: Al Baleed Archaeological City: The ancient city of Al Baleed, located in the Dhofar Governorate, is one of the most prominent historical monuments in Salalah, as it contains the remains of mosques, buildings, docks, ports, in addition to its famous fortress, and the city of Al Baleed flourished in the twelfth and sixteenth centuries AD, and it gained a unique luster in the Islamic era, and this was evident in the design of its old mosque, which is considered one of the most famous ancient mosques in the world.[10] Frankincense Land Museum: The visitor must visit the Frankincense Land Museum, located in the city of Salalah, which is about 5 km from the city center, because it is a museum that includes the time periods of the Sultanate of Oman and its governorates, where the visitor learns about its maritime heritage and historical heritage, through models and archaeological evidence, located in the various halls of the museum.
